    Recognizing excellence and dedication within the Tiv community

    Mzehemen Awards

    The Mzehemen Awards honor individuals who demonstrate exceptional commitment to our core values and make significant contributions to the Tiv community. These awards celebrate achievement, integrity, innovation, and service.

    The Integrity Award

    Recognizes an individual who upholds the highest ethical standards and is dedicated to serving others through transparency and accountability.

    The Young Achiever Award

    Recognises young people's achievements. We invest in their future capabilities as young activists by offering cash prizes, access to project awards, money-can't-buy opportunities, and mentoring.

    The Public Service Award

    Recognises distinguished service to country, state, community, or public, with respect to local custom and considering the different cultural perceptions of public service, through excellence in leadership, service, or humanitarianism.

    The Humanitarian Award

    Recognises the unsung superheroes working in the humanitarian field. We want to celebrate humanitarian workers' outstanding contribution to humanitarian crises.

    The Entrepreneurship Award

    Recognises and celebrates innovative and creative Tiv entrepreneurs who have demonstrated commercial success, growth and impact.

    The Community Impact Award

    Honors the exceptional dedication of Tiv sons and daughters who contribute to the well-being of their communities. Whether through professional or volunteer service, these individuals make a significant and positive impact on the lives of others.

    The Woman of the Year Award

    A prestigious multidisciplinary award that celebrates the outstanding achievements of women. The award aims to raise awareness, recognize, and honor the hard work and valuable contributions of Tiv women.

    Nomination Requirements
    What you need to nominate
    Nominee's personal details

    You'll need your nominee's name, age, address and contact details. You'll also be asked for your nominee's gender.

    Nomination details

    You'll need to write a detailed description explaining why you're nominating them.

    Award type

    Review our award categories and select the appropriate award.

    Evidence of your nominee's contribution

    Any documents which provide evidence of your nominee's contribution. For example, newspaper clippings or letters.

    Supporting letters

    We need two or more letters of support which endorse your nominee's contribution. These should come from people who are familiar with your nominee's services. Supporters can address letters to you as the nominator or to the Awards Committee Secretariat.

    Your contact details

    We need to collect some information about you in case we need to contact you.

    Who deserves the award?

    Honours recognise and celebrate outstanding achievements. There are always fewer honours than people who deserve them, and because they are rare, they should be reserved for people:

    • who have changed things especially by solid, practical achievement;
    • whose work has brought distinction to the Tiv nation or enhanced the Tiv's reputation in their area or activity.

    If you think your nominee demonstrates one or both of these accomplishments and that their contribution stands out from other people's, please consider nominating them for an honour.

    How do I write a nomination?

    The most important thing is to provide evidence of what they've done and how they've made things better for others.

    Your nomination form doesn't need to be a work of art â€“ it doesn't matter whether it's typed or handwritten or whether it has pictures. There isn't a right way or a wrong way to write a nomination and it doesn't need to be particularly formal. There's no "right length". Every nomination is different. But your nomination must tell the story of what your candidate has done.

    Give examples of how they have demonstrated outstanding quality. Show how your candidate has:

    • contributed in a distinctive way to improving the lot of those less able to help themselves
    • devoted themselves to sustained and selfless voluntary service
    • shown innovation or creativity in delivering lasting results

    A good nomination should also describe the difference their contribution has made as vividly and precisely as possible. Try to answer the following questions:

    • How were things before they began?
    • How are they now?
    • What makes your candidate different from others doing the same thing?

    Give details to support these claims. Show how your candidate has:

    • earned the respect of their peers and become a role model in their field
    • produced, perhaps against the odds, sustained achievement which has required moral courage, vision, the ability to make tough choices or determined application and hard work

    As long as they are accurate, do not be afraid of using superlatives in citations. Honours exist specifically to recognise superlative achievement. But remember that superlatives without an explanation are just hot air. Take care always to support any assertions with hard evidence. Do not just say that an achievement has had a widespread effect: describe what that effect has been and show why it has been important.
